# Theoretical & Mathematical Foundations

This document details the mathematical models and metrics utilized in the **MetaFam Knowledge Graph** analysis.

---

## 1. Graph Analysis Metrics

### Centrality Measures
- **Degree Centrality**: The number of connections a node has. Helps identify the most active members.
- **Betweenness Centrality**: Measures how often a node acts as a bridge along the shortest path between two other nodes. High betweenness centrality in family graphs often identifies "connectors" or key generational bridges.
- **Closeness Centrality**: The inverse of the average shortest path length from one node to all others. Indicates how quickly a node can interact with all others in the network.

### Shannon Entropy for Relation Diversity
Shannon Entropy quantifies the diversity of relationships within the graph.
$$H(R) = -\sum_{i=1}^{|R|} p(r_i) \log_2 p(r_i)$$
Where $p(r_i)$ is the probability (relative frequency) of relationship type $r_i$ occurring in the graph. Higher entropy indicates a more varied set of relationship types.

### Density and Clustering Coefficient
- **Density**: The ratio of actual edges to the maximum possible edges. Family graphs typically have low density due to their sparse, hierarchical nature.
- **Clustering Coefficient**: Measures the degree to which nodes tend to cluster together. High values indicate tight-knit family subgroups.

## 2. Community Detection

Community detection algorithms partition the graph into cohesive subgroups.

### Modularity (Louvain Algorithm)
Modularity ($Q$) measures the quality of the network's division into communities by comparing actual intra-community edges to what would be expected in a random network.

$$Q = \frac{1}{2m} \sum_{i,j} \left[ A_{ij} - \frac{k_i k_j}{2m} \right] \delta(c_i, c_j)$$

- $m$: Total number of edges.
- $A_{ij}$: Adjacency matrix element (1 if edge exists, 0 otherwise).
- $k_i, k_j$: Degrees of nodes $i$ and $j$.
- $\delta(c_i, c_j)$: Kronecker delta (1 if nodes are in the same community, 0 otherwise).

### Conductance and Internal Density
- **Conductance**: Measures separation from the rest of the graph. Lower is better (fewer external links).
  $$\text{Conductance}(C) = \frac{\text{Cut}(C)}{\text{Volume}(C)}$$
- **Internal Density**: Measures cohesiveness within the community. Higher is better.
  $$\text{Internal Density}(C) = \frac{2 \cdot |E_{\text{internal}}|}{|C| \cdot (|C| - 1)}$$

### Evaluation Metrics
- **Purity**: Measures how well detected communities align with actual ground-truth families by assigning each community to the most frequent family label.
  $$\text{Purity} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{k=1}^{K} \max_{j} |C_k \cap F_j|$$
- **Normalized Mutual Information (NMI)**: Information-theoretic measure of agreement between predicted communities and true families, normalized to $[0, 1]$.
  $$\text{NMI}(X, Y) = \frac{2 \cdot I(X; Y)}{H(X) + H(Y)}$$
- **Adjusted Rand Index (ARI)**: Measures similarity based on pair-wise groupings, adjusted for chance.

## 3. Rule Mining

Logical rules are extracted to infer new relationships from existing ones.

### Horn Clauses
Horn clauses follow the pattern of logical implications (if A and B, then C).
- **2-hop Pattern**: `rel1(X, Y) âˆ§ rel2(Y, Z) â†’ rel3(X, Z)`
  *(e.g., `motherOf(X, Y) âˆ§ motherOf(Y, Z) â†’ grandmotherOf(X, Z)`)*
- **3-hop Pattern**: `rel1(X, Y) âˆ§ rel2(Y, Z) âˆ§ rel3(Z, W) â†’ rel4(X, W)`

### Inverse Rules
Rules establishing bi-directional or inverse relationships.
- `rel1(X, Y) â†’ rel2(Y, X) âˆ¨ rel3(Y, X)`
  *(e.g., `motherOf(X, Y) â†’ daughterOf(Y, X) âˆ¨ sonOf(Y, X)`)*

### Confidence
Confidence measures how often a rule holds true in the dataset.
$$\text{Confidence}(A \rightarrow B) = \frac{\text{Support}(A \cup B)}{\text{Support}(A)}$$

## 4. Knowledge Graph Embeddings (ComplEx)

The ComplEx model embeds entities and relations into a complex vector space $\mathbb{C}^d$, allowing it to naturally model both symmetric and antisymmetric relations (like parent-child dependencies).

### Scoring Function
The score for a triple $(h, r, t)$ is the real part of the multi-linear dot product:
$$\text{score} = \text{Re}(\langle \mathbf{h}, \mathbf{r}, \bar{\mathbf{t}} \rangle)$$
Expanded as:
$$\text{score} = h_{re} \cdot r_{re} \cdot t_{re} + h_{im} \cdot r_{re} \cdot t_{im} + h_{re} \cdot r_{im} \cdot t_{im} - h_{im} \cdot r_{im} \cdot t_{re}$$
Where vectors have real ($re$) and imaginary ($im$) components, and $\bar{\mathbf{t}}$ is the complex conjugate of $\mathbf{t}$.

### Loss Function and Negative Sampling
The model is trained using **Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** by contrasting true triples from the dataset against false (negative) triples generated by corrupting the head or tail entity.
